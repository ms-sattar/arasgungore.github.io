My Teaching Philosophy is

I am a Teacher who firmly believes that every student has something valuable to share in the S.T.E.M. classroom. I aim to increase the studentâ€™s confidence and interest in the use of Technology. I truly believe that creating opportunities in the classroom will help my students to think analytically and enhance their problem-solving approach. Learning various tools, skills, and techniques will inspire my students to feel confident working with technology.

My intention is to create a safe space in the classroom for every child where they can learn, make mistakes, and have a voice. Also, every child is unique and has different capabilities of learning, so they need support for their growth on an individual basis. I believe in making my classroom inclusive using adaptive strategies and minimizing social, physical, and emotional barriers to learning. I tend towards multiple approaches to assessment, as I look forward to seeing different ways in which various students can excel. Creating rubrics and providing them with comprehensive feedback will help them to overcome their problems. Also, their anonymous feedback in various forms like exit slips, google forums, etc., will help me to better understand my teaching skills.

I will use my intentional approach to ensure all students must be heard and seen in the classroom. Morning meetings, listening to them deeply, and engaging them in various activities that have elements of fun e.g., Kahoot, puzzles, games, etc., can help me to observe their behavior and will help me to make better connections with them.

Depending upon the subject and content to be taught, I will use the Universal Design of Learning and Inquiry-based learning. Instead of using the traditional instructing style, I want to make my classroom a Thinking Classroom. I will guide them to think critically about every problem and develop new habits of thought and reflection.

Finally, this is just the beginning. As I will have more experience, my teaching philosophy will be evolved. Learning is a continuous process, and I am excited to be a part of it.
